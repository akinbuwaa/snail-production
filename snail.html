MORPHOLOGY OF SNAIL

  • Snails consists mainly of two parts; the body and the shell.
  • The body is divided into three; the head, the foot and the visceral mass.
  • The head carries two pairs of retractable tentacles.
  • The upper and longer tentacles carries the impaired eyes while the lower one is the olfactory clue.
  • The hump-shaped visceral mass is housed in the shell above the foot
  • The visceral mass contains the digestive, reproductive, and respiratory organs
  • In most of the species, the shell accounts for about a third of the body weight.
  • The skin over the visceral hump secretes a large calcareous shell(98% CaCO3)

SPECIES OF SNAIL

Species of snails under this reveiw are: ACHATINA ACHATINA(Ghana Snail)

  • A large snail which grow up to 30cm in length.
  • The shell colour is Mustard brown with a characteristic black zigzag stripes pattern.
  • The shell end is pointed.
  • They reproduce by self fertilization(RECIPROCAL MATING) mainly in the rainy seasons.
  • They lay eggs in clutches of 30-300 eggs.
  • Hatching of eggs takes place within 10 to 21 days depending on temperature.
  • They have high hatchability of 40-50%.
  • They are sexually mature at about 6-7 months of age.

ACHACHATINA MARGINATA A.M ( NIGERIA SNAIL)

  • Also known as the big black snail
  • There 2 types AMO & AMS.
  • It can weigh up to 500g live weight
  • The shell is less pointed than other AA.
  • The roundness of shell especially obvious in young animals.
  • Growth is found to be proportional to rainfall and humidity
  • Reaches point of lay at 7-8 months
  • The number of eggs per clutch is low, about 4 to 8 eggs.
  • Incubation is between 21-28 days

ACHATINA FULICA (COMPOUND SNAIL)

  • It can reach up to 20cm in length or more.
  • The shell is conical and spiral, brown with weak darker banded marks across the spiral.
  • Shell colouration is highly variable depending on diet.
  • Mature weight is about 250g.
  • They reach sexual maturity in less than a year.
  • Reciprocal copulation must occur to produce viable eggs.
  • Within 300 to 1000 eggs are laid per year depending on length of raining season.

SNAIL HOUSING

There are 3 different types of snail housing systems namely:

  1. ensive system.
  2. Semi-Intensive system
  3. Extensive system(Free range/Green house)

INTENSIVE SYSTEM

  1. It is also known as low fenced/wooden cage/battery cage.
  2. It allows the farmer to monitor the activity of the snails e.g. feeding, incubation, hatching, nursery operations e.t.c. in their various cells.

EXTENSIVE SNAIL HOUSING SYSTEM

  • This is equally known as free range or outdoor housing system(Green House)
  • A space of land is nurtured to emulate the natural habitat snails will thrive
  • This is the best housing system for commercial snail farming.

SEMI INTENSIVE HOUSING SYSTEM

  • This system is mostly adapted by the small scale snail farmers.
  • It includes the use of buckets or drum, wooden boxes, tires, baskets e.t.c.

WHERE AND HOW TO GET THE SUITABLE SPECIES OF SNAIL FOR FARMING

    Buying of foundation stock from the market Is not advisable because of the following reasons;
  • Snails at the market have been exposed to direct sunlight and heat which might lead to loss of fertility.
  • Exposure to high rate of wind which may lead to dehydration, loss of fertility and even high mortality.
  • Older snail, lesser eggs; snails in the market are meant for consumption and not farming.

SNAIL SOIL AND SOIL TREATMENT

    Snail soil;
  • Loamy soil is the best soil for snail farming.
  • Loamy soil is high in organic matter which ensures adequate relation of water and nutrients.
    • Soil Treatment;
  • The loamy soil should be sterilized to kill insects and micro organism.
  • Mulching materials include dry leaves; dry plantain/banana/cocoa leaves
  • The mulching materials should equally be sterilized in intensive housing system.

FOOD AND FEEDING TECHNIQUE

  • Snails are essentially herbivores.
  • Snails require carbohydrates for energy and protein for growth.
  • Snails require large amount of calcium for their shell formation, as well a vitamins and minerals.
  • Foods that are dangerous to snails include contaminated food, particularly the ones treated with pesticide or salt are potential snail killer.
  • Starchy food like rice, millet and pasta are not easily digestible and might end up blocking the digestive system(Bloating).
    • NOTE: SALT AND CHLORINATED WATER MUST BE AVOIDED
  • Snail formulated ration can also be introduced as SUPPLEMENTS.

REPRODUCTION IN SNAIL AND EGG INCUBATION

NURSERY MANAGEMENT

  • Feed the hatchlings with only vegetables, fruits and calcium from the remaining egg shell they hatched from.
  • Spray water on the hatchling twice daily.
  • Always keep record of your snail(Number of eggs and hatchlings)
  • Clean the pen daily.
  • Spend quality time with your baby snail, experience is the best teacher.
